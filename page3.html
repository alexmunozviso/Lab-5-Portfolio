Tales from a City Ripped in Half

Tales from a City Ripped in Half is a personal interpretation of the map of my hometown, Sant Feliu de Llobregat; a small city in the Metropolitan Area of Barcelona.

The map narrates a story of urban experience subdued to transport infrastructure. It is also a memorial to the ways in which, for decades, people have suffered, resisted, and adapted to a railway line ripping city landscape and city lives apart. This story is a very personal one and, as such, is narrated as a digital scrapbook halfway betwen a diary and a zine.
